Announcements & Updates

Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) and National Stock Exchange (NSE)

These are the two primary stock exchanges in India. BSE is the oldest stock exchange in Asia, established in 1875, while NSE was founded in 1992.

Key Indices

The two major stock market indices in India are the BSE Sensex and the NSE Nifty. These indices track the performance of a select group of stocks and are considered barometers of the Indian stock market's overall health.

Volatility

The Indian stock market can be highly volatile, influenced by domestic factors such as economic data, corporate earnings, and government policies, as well as global events like geopolitical tensions and fluctuations in international markets.

Market Regulations

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is the regulatory body overseeing the Indian securities market. SEBI regulates stock exchanges, brokers, merchant banks, and other intermediaries in the market to ensure fair and transparent trading practices.

Market Hours

The Indian stock market operates from Monday to Friday, with trading hours typically from 9:15 AM to 3:30 PM Indian Standard Time (IST). However, there are pre-opening and post-closing sessions as well.

Market Capitalization

The Indian stock market boasts companies with diverse market capitalizations, ranging from large-cap to small-cap stocks. Some of India's largest companies by market capitalization are in sectors like IT, banking, and energy.

Foreign Institutional Investors

These are major players in the Indian stock market. FIIs refer to foreign entities investing in Indian securities, while DIIs comprise domestic entities such as mutual funds, insurance companies, and banks.

Budget Day

The annual Union Budget presentation by the Indian government is a significant event for the stock market. Investors keenly watch for announcements related to taxes, fiscal policies, infrastructure spending, and other economic reforms.
